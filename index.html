How it works

Basic idea of ViDock

Using ViDock is quick and easy without the need of any tools. Insert a PCIe Card into ViDock and connect it to your computer.

The right PCIe card for the right Job

How can ViDock make your small PC 1000% faster

The answer is PCIe Expansion, which is expanding your Computer’s ability with PCIe Add In Cards, or short PCIe Cards.

How a Computer works

Today’s CPUs and motherboard have all functions integrated: GPU and RAID are integrated in the CPU. A sound chip is on the motherboard. These integrated functions are low cost, but also low performance in comparison what a dedicated PCIe Add In Card can deliver.

How to make a Desktop 1000% faster

There is one thing that Desktop Computer have and all other Computer form factors do not have. It is why all high performance workstations are Desktops: They have PCIe Slots. In case you need high performance for a particular application, you add the corresponding PCIe Add In Card into the empty PCIe Slot. A high end PCIe Add In Card can easily outperform the CPU by 1000%. In doing so you also reduce the workload / bottleneck on the CPU. Specialized PCIe Cards are also more energy efficient than the generic CPU.

ViDock does exactly the same thing

ViDock works exactly the same way, except for one change: The PCIe signals are not transferred over PCB traces but via the V²Cable. This change allows to have two separate housings: One for the computer and one for the PCIe Add In Card.

Example in real life: Mini Computer and ViDock

The Mini Computer (PCIe Host) with a V² Port connects with V² Cable to ViDock (PCIe Expansion Target). One strengths of V² is its backwards compatibility to connect to existing computers, explained in Breaking through the PCIe Connection Barrier

Breaking through PCIe Barriers

What ViDock does is not new. It is called a PCI(e) Expansion Enclosure. This has been around for over two decades. What is new: ViDock makes PCIe Expansion technology accessible to the consumer, by breaking through its barriers.

Breaking through Price Barrier

Not long ago (2005) all PCIe Expansion Enclosures were north of $800 US. ViDock G2 started a price revolution to be the first PCIe enclosure under $200 US. ViDock G6 is doing it again, being the first PCIe enclosure under $100 US with Early Bird pricing as low as $69 US.

Breaking through the Speed Barrier

ViDock G6 uses V²C Gen 2 PCIe cable connection with x4 PCIe. 400% faster PCIe connection than in previous generations of ViDock.

Picking the right ViDock for you!

Pick ViDock PCIe G6 when …

… you want a very small PCIe Expansion Enclosure. You will have no trouble to find space for it or share it with different computers. It will fit all PCIe cards, unless they are oversized or need additional power other than what the PCIe slot can provide. In such case choose ViDock GPU.

Pick ViDock GPU G6 when …

… ViDock PCIe is too small or when you want to use an ATX Power Supply. There are two reasons when your PCIe Card requires ViDock GPU instead of ViDock PCIe:

  1. Your PCIe Card needs additional power plugged in with cables
  2. Your PCIe Card has oversized dimensions, typically large GPU coolers

ViDock GPU is significantly larger than ViDock PCIe. It still can be used in a Modular PCIe Expansion setup, where you share the same PCIe Card with various computer. It has a comfortable carry grip.

Pick ViDock GPU Cheapo G6 when …

… you want all of the goodness of ViDock GPU, but you want to save money and you do not care so much about the looks of the housing. All the inners are high quality and identical to ViDock GPU G6, but the housing materials are made of light plastics. They will do their job nicely, but it will feel, well, cheap. So be warned. If you want a quality housing made of sandblasted and anodized Aluminum, go with ViDock GPU or ViDock PCIe. But hey, with ViDock GPU Cheapo you save the money. Not only on the ViDock itself, but also on the shipping cost. ViDock GPU Cheapo housing is half weight!

FAQ ViDock

Why no direct Thunderbolt Interface for ViDock?

Because an external Thunderbolt Adapter saves your money. It is doing it in several ways:

  1. A Thunderbolt interface is very expensive. By moving it into an external adapter you can share the same Thunderbolt interface with several ViDock.
  2. If you change your Host Computer later and no longer use Thunderbolt, then you do not need to buy a new ViDock, only a new V²C Cable.
  3. If we had integrated the Thunderbolt Interface into ViDock, we would need to develop and manufacture a different ViDock for every different host port. That would make ViDock more expensive. Instead now we only have one model of ViDock and you adopt to the host port connector with V²C Cable / V²A Adapter, in this case the Thunderbolt Adapter.
  4. Until today Thunderbolt does not support external GPU on any OS and PCIe Expansion on Windows. As such we can not get a Thunderbolt License to develop a ViDock with integrated Thunderbolt Interface.

How to connect a Thunderbolt Computer to ViDock today?

The Sonnet Echo ExpressCard Adapter has been proven by our customers as a high performing option for connecting a Thunderbolt Host Computer to a ViDock. With a ViDock it can even outperform the Sonnet PCIe Expansion Box with Thunderbolt built in as reviewed by Nino in his comparison Benchmark Review: http://www.notebookcheck.net/Review-External-Graphics-Cards-for-Laptops.112162.0.html

Isn’t 10,000 USD a bit low budget to develop ViDock G6

Indeed. Those who followed us on facebook know that we have been developing all of this since summer 2011. Development of ViDock G6 is mostly finished and has been financed thanks to the support of our Facebook community buying ViDock. But in order to significantly lower the cost of ViDock we need to increase production quantity so we can benefit from economies of scale. This is how you are helping us by backing this campaign and in return we can provide you with a low cost ViDock G6.

Why not keep ViDock G5 in production?

ViDock G5 only connects to ExpressCard. After ExpressCard was phased out, sales of ViDock G5 have dropped so much that it was not economical to keep it in production any longer. We will of course continue technical support for our existing ViDock customers.

Why do you tie the publication of V² to a Stretch Goal

We have finished development of the technology of V² and have sufficient documentation for internal use. Publishing a standard with sufficient documentation that anybody can easily use it is a lot of extra work. The stretch goal does two things: A) it tells us if there is an interest for it B) it provides the necessary funding to complete the extra work.

Why is V²Configurator the first published V² project

  • ViDock, ViCase and V² offer many configuration options. The V² Configurator greatly simplifies the configuration process, assisting the Indiegogo Campaigns
  • It is a community development project. Changing from our current server solution to GitHub makes it easier for others to contribute.

How does V² integrate with other Open Source Communities?

ViDock G6 and V² is developed with:

  • Arduino Entry Level MCU IDE, RDKs, lots of reference projects and wonderful community
  • Atmel Studio High End MCU IDE
  • Fritzing Entry level 2 Layer PCB Prototyping, Design and Production
  • KiCAD High End PCB Design for Multilayer and Impedance matched PCBs
  • Eclipse High End IDE for Java / C / C++ projects
  • FreeCAD Free and Open Source parametric 3D CAD Modeler and Designer
  • Git Free and Open Source distributed version control system
  • GitHub Powerful collaboration, code review and code management for Open Source projects
  • Joomla Web Site Content Management System.
